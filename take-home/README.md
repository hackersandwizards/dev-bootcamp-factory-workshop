# Take your factory home

Two steps. The first carries the factory's spine off the calculator and onto a
real project. The second adds the loop that makes it improve. They mirror the
two halves of the bootcamp: Day 1 built the spine, Day 2 built the loop.

## Start here

If you are in the session, open [EXERCISE.md](EXERCISE.md). It is the timed,
step-by-step guide for both days. The two folders below are the mechanics it
refers to.

## starter/ - the spine (Day 1 free slots, and Monday)

The planner, refine and implement factory, generalized off the calculator, with
observability already on. Spin it up as a new repo, scaffold a real project, and
run a work item through all three stations. See starter/README.md.

## instrument/ - the loop (after the Day 2 retrospective)

Evals that tell a good Bean from a bad one, and one guard that runs on every
implement. Use it once you have done the Day 2 morning exercises. See
instrument/README.md.

## The one thing to take away

The calculator was the rig. The factory is the product. The skills you point at
your own codebase on Monday are the same ones you built here. The contract and
the guardrails carry over unchanged. Only the build and test commands are
project specific.
